JASPER’S WORLD — CAMERA & WORLD ANCHOR CONTRACT GUARDRAIL
(Parallax, Camera Attachment, Frame-of-Reference, and VFX Exceptions)

PURPOSE
-------
This guardrail defines strict rules governing how visual entities relate to
the camera, the world, and player perception.

Its goal is to prevent camera-follow artifacts, jarring parallax motion,
frame-of-reference errors, and accidental regressions when adding biomes,
VFX, or editor tooling.

This guardrail governs CAMERA RELATIONSHIPS and REFERENCE FRAMES.

================================================================
CORE PRINCIPLE
================================================================

EVERY VISUAL ENTITY MUST HAVE EXACTLY ONE FRAME OF REFERENCE.

An entity must be classified as ONE of the following:
- Camera-attached (screen-space)
- World-anchored (static)
- World-anchored with parallax (delta-based)
- Camera-relative world-effect (VFX exception)

Hybrid or ambiguous anchoring is forbidden.

================================================================
ENTITY CATEGORIES (MANDATORY)
================================================================

1) CAMERA-ATTACHED ENTITIES (SCREEN-SPACE)
------------------------------------------
Examples:
- UI
- HUD
- Screen-space effects (vignette, fade, overlays)

Rules:
- May follow camera transform directly
- May parent to the camera
- May recompute absolute position from camera position

Camera-attached entities MUST be explicitly documented.

------------------------------------------
2) WORLD-ANCHORED ENTITIES (NO PARALLAX)
------------------------------------------
Examples:
- Platforms
- Enemies
- Ground props
- Interactive objects

Rules:
- Spawn once in world-space
- Must NEVER read camera position to set world position
- Must NEVER parent to the camera
- Movement derives only from gameplay or physics systems

------------------------------------------
3) WORLD-ANCHORED PARALLAX ENTITIES
------------------------------------------
Examples:
- Trees
- Buildings
- Terrain silhouettes
- Large environment layers

Rules:
- Spawn once at a fixed world-space anchor
- Retain original anchor position
- MUST NOT recompute absolute position from camera position
- MUST use delta-based motion ONLY

Allowed:
- position += camera_delta * parallax_factor

Forbidden:
- position = camera.position * factor
- Parenting to camera

If the camera does not move, parallax entities must not move.

------------------------------------------
4) CAMERA-RELATIVE WORLD EFFECTS (VFX EXCEPTION)
------------------------------------------
Examples:
- Weather particles (rain, snow)
- Fog motes
- Wind-blown leaves
- Ambient dust

These are NOT world structure and do not convey spatial truth.
They are perceptual effects intended to maintain consistent density
around the player.

Rules:
- MAY spawn volumes relative to camera position
- MUST NOT parent directly to the camera transform
- Once spawned, particles move independently in world-space
- MUST NOT use parallax math or depth illusion logic
- MUST NOT occlude gameplay silhouettes or readability

All camera-relative VFX systems MUST be:
- Explicitly documented
- Tagged (e.g. CameraRelativeVfx)

================================================================
VISUAL SANITY RULE
================================================================

If a non-UI object moves when the camera is stationary,
its anchoring is incorrect.

This is a blocking defect.

================================================================
FOREST / LOW-INTENSITY BIOME GUIDANCE
================================================================

For tutorial or calm biomes (e.g. Forest):
- Parallax differences between layers must be subtle
- Motion should suggest mass and calm, not spectacle
- Motion should never draw attention to itself

Aggressive parallax is reserved for later biomes (City, Sanctuary).

================================================================
CAMERA QUERY RULE
================================================================

All parallax and world-anchored systems MUST explicitly bind to:
- GameplayCamera

Generic queries (With<Camera>) are forbidden.

================================================================
AI SAFETY RULE
================================================================

AI agents MUST:
- Never attach world structure to the camera
- Never compute absolute world position from camera position
- Preserve original spawn transforms as anchors
- Treat camera-relative VFX as a documented exception only

================================================================
FINAL RULE
================================================================

IF A NON-UI, NON-VFX ENTITY FEELS "GLUED" TO THE CAMERA,
THE CONTRACT HAS BEEN VIOLATED.

END OF CAMERA & WORLD ANCHOR CONTRACT GUARDRAIL
