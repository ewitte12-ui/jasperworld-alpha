JASPER’S WORLD — BACKGROUND & LAYERING VISUAL GUARDRAIL
(Bevy 0.18, 2.5D side‑scrolling game)

PURPOSE
-------
This document is a NON‑NEGOTIABLE GUARDRAIL for background composition, parallax layers,
and scene coverage. It exists to permanently eliminate horizontal bars, seams between
background layers, clear‑color bleed, and other visual artifacts that appear inside the
play space (including bars touching gameplay sprites).

This guardrail addresses ONLY issues that were clearly observed or strongly indicated
by the shared scene image.

================================================================
STOP CONDITIONS (DO NOT SKIP)
================================================================
Apply this guardrail immediately if ANY of the following appear:
- A horizontal bar or band appears inside the scene
- A bar touches or overlaps gameplay sprites
- Background color changes abruptly at a straight horizontal line
- ClearColor is visible between background layers
- Visual seams appear as the camera moves vertically

================================================================
BACKGROUND & LAYERING GUARDRAIL — ORDERED CHECKLIST
================================================================

[1] FULL CAMERA COVERAGE (MANDATORY)
-----------------------------------
EVERY background layer MUST fully cover the camera’s visible height.

Rules:
- Background quad height MUST exceed camera view height
- NEVER rely on two layers “meeting exactly” at an edge
- Layers MUST overlap vertically by a safety margin

Reason:
- Exact edge‑to‑edge matching creates visible seams
- Camera motion, parallax, or scaling exposes gaps immediately

================================================================
[2] LAYER OVERLAP REQUIREMENT (MANDATORY)
----------------------------------------
Adjacent background layers MUST overlap.

Rules:
- Upper layer MUST extend below the lower layer’s top
- Lower layer MUST extend above the upper layer’s bottom

DO NOT:
- Stack backgrounds so edges merely touch

Reason:
- Prevents clear‑color bleed‑through
- Eliminates horizontal “bars” inside the scene

================================================================
[3] CAMERA‑AWARE BACKGROUND SIZE (MANDATORY)
--------------------------------------------
Background geometry MUST be sized using camera metrics.

Rules:
- Background height >= camera vertical span + margin
- Margin MUST tolerate worst‑case parallax offset

Reason:
- Backgrounds must be stable under camera movement
- Camera framing is the dominant constraint, not art bounds

================================================================
[4] PARALLAX SCALING SAFETY (MANDATORY)
--------------------------------------
If parallax or scaled transforms are used:

Rules:
- Scaling a background layer smaller REQUIRES increasing its base size
- Parallax layers MUST assume reduced effective coverage

Reason:
- Parallax transforms shrink visible coverage
- Unadjusted layers reveal clear color as horizontal bands

================================================================
[5] SINGLE PROJECTION FOR BACKGROUNDS (MANDATORY)
-------------------------------------------------
All background layers MUST share the same camera projection.

DO NOT:
- Render different background layers with different camera projections
- Mix FixedVertical and AutoMin across background cameras

Reason:
- Mismatched projections cause seams and apparent bars

================================================================
[6] CLEAR COLOR SAFETY RULE (MANDATORY)
--------------------------------------
The ClearColor MUST NEVER be relied on for visual coverage.

Rules:
- ClearColor may be visible ONLY outside the playable world
- ClearColor MUST NOT appear between background layers

Debug test:
- Temporarily set ClearColor to a bright color
- If it appears inside the scene, background coverage is wrong

================================================================
[7] BACKGROUND VERTICAL HEADROOM (MANDATORY)
--------------------------------------------
Backgrounds MUST extend above the tallest reachable gameplay height.

Rules:
- Background top > maximum player jump + camera offset
- Do NOT align background top with platform peaks

Reason:
- Gameplay dictates background bounds, not initial framing

================================================================
[8] DEPTH & DRAW ORDER CONSISTENCY (MANDATORY)
----------------------------------------------
Backgrounds MUST follow a strict depth ordering.

Rules:
- Sky furthest back
- Distant mountains behind mid‑layers
- No Z overlap between different background concepts

Reason:
- Overlapping or identical depths can cause unexpected visual seams

================================================================
[9] DEBUGGING RULE (MANDATORY)
------------------------------
When diagnosing visual bars:

Steps:
- Change ClearColor to a bright color
- Toggle individual background layers
- Temporarily over‑scale the suspected layer

If the bar disappears → coverage failure confirmed

================================================================
FINAL RULE
================================================================
If a horizontal band or seam is visible INSIDE the scene:

→ THIS BACKGROUND & LAYERING GUARDRAIL MUST BE APPLIED
→ BEFORE investigating camera math, sprites, UVs, or atlases

Rendering stability can expose background composition errors.
These layers must be robust by construction.

END OF BACKGROUND GUARDRAIL
